Differential  localization  of  MAPK activated  protein  kinases  RSK and  MSK in  mouse  brain RSK and  MSK are  closely  related  members  of  the  MAP kinase  activated  kinase  family  and  are  direct  substrates  and  effectors  of  the  well  studied  mitogen  activated  protein  kinases  Although  extensively  characterized  at  the  biochemical  level  little  is  known  about  the  localization  of  these  protein  kinases  in  the  brain We  utilized  immunohistochemistry  to  determine  the  cellular  and  subcellular  localization  of  RSK and  MSK in  the  adult  mouse  brain RSK is  expressed  at  highest  levels  in  cerebellum especially  in  granule neurons and  within  neuropil  of  the  molecular layer RSK is  also  expressed  in  microglia  throughout  the  brain In  a  focal  trauma  model  RSK immunoreactivity  is  increased  in  activated  microglia  RSK expression  is  also  prominent  in  many  large pyramidal neurons throughout  the  brain At  the  subcellular  level  RSK is  highly  concentrated  in  the  golgi  apparatus  of  both  neurons and  astroglia  In  contrast  MSK is  expressed  at  highest  levels  in  striatal and olfactory tubercle neurons and  to  a  lesser  degree  in  cerebellar Purkinje cells MSK is  also  expressed  in  a  subset  of  astroglia  At  the  subcellular  level  MSK is  confined  to  the  nucleus  of  all  expressing cell types  The  differential  cellular  and  subcellular  localizations  of  RSK and  MSK suggest  divergent  functional  roles  in  the  brain with  RSK poised  to  regulate  membrane  trafficking  or  membrane  localized  signaling  and  MSK involved  in  modification  of  nuclear  histones  and  transcription  factors
