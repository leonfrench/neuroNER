Reversal  of  pancreatitis  induced  pain  by  an  orally  available  small molecule  interleukin receptor antagonist  Pancreatic  pain  resulting  from  chronic  inflammation  of  the  pancreas  is  often  intractable  and  clinically  difficult  to  manage  with  available  analgesics  reflecting  the  need  for  more  effective  therapies  The  mechanisms  underlying  pancreatitis  pain  are  not  well  understood  Here  the  possibility  that  interleukin IL may  promote  pancreatitis  pain  was  investigated  with  TB O  formyl  R  epoxyresibufogenin  EBRF a  small molecule  IL receptor antagonist  that  was  semi  synthetically  derived  from  natural  sources  The  potential  activity  and  mechanism  of  TB were  investigated  following  the  induction  of  persistent  pancreatitis  using  dibutyltin  dichloride  DBTC in  rats  TB displaces  the  binding  of  IL to  the  human  recombinant  soluble  IL receptor with  apparent  high  affinity  and  inhibits  IL mediated  cell growth  Systemic  or  oral  but  not  intrathecal  administration  of  TB reversed  DBTC induced  abdominal  hypersensitivity  in  a  dose  and  time  dependent  manner  IL levels  were  significantly  up  regulated  in  the  dorsal root  ganglia  DRG of  rats  with  pancreatitis  on  day  after  DBTC injection  IL enhanced  capsaicin  evoked  release  of  calcitonin gene  related  peptide  from  cultured  DRG neurons was  blocked  by  TB Our  data  demonstrate  that  TB acts  as  a  systemically  available  and  orally  active  small molecule  IL receptor antagonist  TB effectively  reduces  pancreatitis  induced  pain  through  peripheral  mechanisms  that  are  likely  due  to  a  increased  expression  of  IL in  the  DRG and  b  IL mediated  sensitization  of  nociceptive  neurons The  activity  of  TB implicates  an  important  role  for  IL in  sustaining  pancreatitis  pain  Strategies  targeting  IL actions  through  small molecule  antagonists  may  offer  novel  approaches  to  improve  the  therapy  of  chronic  pancreatitis  and  other  chronic  pain  states
